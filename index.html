Here are some tips for being a responsible cat owner:
  • Keep your cat indoors Cats that roam freely outdoors may have shorter lives, while indoor cats can live up to 15 years or more.
  • Clean their liter box daily A clean litter box helps your cat's health and prevents them from eliminating in inappropriate places
  • Consider spaying or neutering Spaying and neutering your cat can help control the pet population.
  • Microchipping Microchipping is part of responsible pet ownership and can help reunite your cat if they get lost, stolen, or injured.
  • Give your cat regular exercise Regular exercise helps your cat maintain body tone and muscle.
  • You can play with your cat using dangle toys and teasers, or by chasing them around the house.
  • Invest in a scratching post Cats need an outlet for their scratching urges and a scratching post can also help protect your furniture, drapery, and carpet.
  • Create an enticing indoor environment Cats can get bored, so create an enticing indoor environment for your cat with condos cat trees, window perches, and treats hidden around the house.
